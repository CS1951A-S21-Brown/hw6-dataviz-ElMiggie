Accessible Data Visualization

4. My dashboard could use several improvements to improve accessibility. I'd say anyone without a visual disability could understand the dashboard and graphs. However for those with disabilties such as Tritanomaly color-blindness, my graphs (namely the pie chart) would be difficult to interpret. Changing the current color scheme with higher contrasting colors would go a long way in helping these users see my graphs. Furthermore, all three of graphs would be completely inaccessible for blind users, as such, utilizing sonification to demonstrate the values of each bar audibly would allow even blind users to interpret the data.
5. The time crunch to complete assignment definitely contributed to me not implementing more user accessible features. In addition, the extra amount of work required to implement seemingly non-noticeable features also discourage me. This is emblomatic of the reasons accessible features are not typically pursued--some engineers just want to do the bare minimum and as long as the end result looks good to them, then they're willing to publish their work.
